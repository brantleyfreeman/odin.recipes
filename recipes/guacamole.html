Guacamole

You can make this quacamole recipe smooth or chunky depending on your tastes!

Guacamole is an avacado-based dip, spread, or salad that was first developed in Mexico. In addition to its modern Mexican cuisine, it has become part of international and American cuisine as a dip, condiment and salad ingredient

As the major ingredient of guacamole is raw avacado, the nutrional value of the dish derives from avocado vitamins, minerals and fats, providing dietary fiber, several B vitamins, vitamin K, vitamin E, and potassium in significant content

Ingredients

  • 3 Avocados - peeled, pitted, and mashed
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 roma (plum) tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 pinch ground cayenne pepper (optional)

Directions

  1. In a medium sized bowl, mash together the avocados
  2. Next, add your lime juice and salt
  3. After that dice up your onions, cilantro, and tomatoes. Mix these ingredients in the bowl
  4. Stir in your cayenne pepper and minced garlic
  5. And lastly, refrigerate 1 hour for the best flavor!
